I was the lead developer on The Post’s [“American Icon” series](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/ar-15-america-gun-culture-politics/), a year-long examination of the AR-15’s role in American life. Working with a UX designer, I built the series' visual identity: interactive art-directed toppers, persistent series navigation, and components like dividers and photo packages.

This is a full list of stories in the package.

- [The gun that divides a nation](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/ar-15-america-gun-culture-politics/)
- [The Blast Effect](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/ar-15-damage-to-human-body/)
- [The policing paradox](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/police-ar-15-gun-control/)
- [Why do Americans own AR-15s?](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/american-ar-15-gun-owners/)
- [The radicals' rifle](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/ar-15-armed-extremist-militia-groups/)
- [A Southern town embraces its AR-15 factory](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/gun-factory-economy-ar-15/)
- [Varmints, soldiers and looming threats: See the ads used to sell the AR-15](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/interactive/2023/history-of-ar-15-marketing/)
- [High-capacity-magazine bans could save lives. Will they hold up in court?](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/high-capacity-magazine-ban-legal-challenges/)
- [Terror on repeat](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/ar-15-force-mass-shootings/)
- [After Sandy Hook, they voted no. Now these senators want new gun laws.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2023/assault-weapons-ban-debate-gun-control-ar-15-mass-shootings/)

For [ “The Blast Effect,”](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/ar-15-damage-to-human-body/) the most technically ambitious story, we built a scroll-driven video that showed readers, frame by frame, how an AR-15 bullet destroys the human body. It included fallbacks for mobile video, loading states, device and orientation detection, and error handling, so the story worked on every device and browser, including low-power mode.

I also earned a byline on [“Why do Americans own AR-15s?”](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/interactive/2023/american-ar-15-gun-owners/) for the graphics I contributed.

The series won several awards, including the [2024 Pulitzer Prize for National Reporting](https://www.pulitzer.org/winners/staff-washington-post-0).
